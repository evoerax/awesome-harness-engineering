# Office Hours

YC Office Hours — reframe your product idea before writing code.

## Two Modes

**Startup Mode:** For real products. Six forcing questions.
**Builder Mode:** For side projects, hackathons, learning. Design thinking brainstorming.

## Six Forcing Questions (Startup Mode)

1. **Demand Reality** — Who specifically needs this? How do you know?
2. **Status Quo** — How are they solving this today? What's broken?
3. **Desperate Specificity** — What's the ONE thing it must do on day one?
4. **Narrowest Wedge** — What's the smallest thing you can ship tomorrow?
5. **Observation** — How will you know if it's working?
6. **Future-Fit** — If this succeeds, what does year 2 look like?

## Process

1. Understand the user's idea
2. Challenge assumptions (not just accept them)
3. Ask questions one at a time
4. Propose 3 implementation approaches
5. Recommend the narrowest wedge
6. Write design doc

## Anti-Patterns

- Accepting the user's framing without questioning
- Building everything at once
- Skipping "who needs this?" to jump to "how to build this"
